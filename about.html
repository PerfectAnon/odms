About HeartGive

Building trust between donors and the causes they care about

Our Story

We Started With One Simple Belief

HeartGive was founded in 2022 with a single mission — to make charitable giving as transparent, simple, and impactful as possible. We saw a world full of willing donors and genuine need, but a wide trust gap standing between them.

Today, we are a cloud-based donation platform that bridges that gap. We verify every NGO on our platform, track every rupee donated, and give donors a window into the real-world impact of their contributions.

We are not just a payment processor. We are a transparency engine for the charity ecosystem in India.

₹2.4 Cr+ Total funds raised
380+ Verified NGO partners
18,000+ Lives directly impacted
What Drives Us

Mission, Vision & Values

Our purpose is guided by a clear set of principles that shape every decision we make.

Our Mission

To digitise and democratise charitable giving in India — making it secure, transparent, and accessible to every donor, regardless of location or technical expertise.

Our Vision

A nation where no verified NGO goes unfunded for lack of donor trust, and every donor can trace their contribution from payment to real-world impact.

Radical Transparency

We publish a public Impact Ledger. Every donation is logged on-chain, receipts are auto-generated, and impact stories are shared by NGOs directly with donors.

Uncompromised Trust

NGOs go through a rigorous verification process including legal document review before they are allowed to list campaigns on our platform.

Our Principles

What We Stand For

Security

PCI-DSS compliant payments. We never touch raw card data.

Speed

Donations reach campaigns in real-time. No delays, no middlemen.

Sustainability

We operate a paperless model, reducing carbon footprint of charity work.

Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant. Designed for donors of all abilities.

Accountability

NGOs must post impact stories after campaigns close or funds are withheld.

Compassion

Every feature we build asks one question: does this serve the donor or the cause?
